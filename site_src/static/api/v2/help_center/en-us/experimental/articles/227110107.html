If your organization is using McAfee or MX Logic, you will need to\u00a0whitelist KnowBe4's IPs as \"Allowed Senders\" to allow our phishing and training-related emails through to your end users.\u00a0You can also whitelist by our mail server hostnames in this area, although we recommend IP in most cases.
\n
You can modify your policy-level Allowed Senders list by following the steps below. This new policy will allow any inbound mail flow originating from those\u00a0three IP addresses to bypass the spam and content filters in McAfee/MX Logic.\u00a0
\n
The instructions below are for a third-party software. If you run into issues whitelisting KnowBe4 in McAfee/MXLogic,\u00a0we recommend reaching out to McAfee/MX Logic for specific instructions. You can also\u00a0contact our support team\u00a0whenever you need assistance.
\n
    \n
  1. Login to the Control Console. Select the Email Protection tab, and then the Policies tab.
    2. \n
  2. Highlight the inbound policy you wish to change and click Edit.
    3. \n
  3. Click the Allow/Deny tab, then add our IP addresses to the Add Address box. For the most up-to-date list of our IP addresses, see the KnowBe4's IP Addresses, Hostnames, and Headers section of our Whitelisting Data and Anti-Spam Information article.\u00a0
    4. \n
  4. Enable the checkbox for Bypass attachment policy if you plan on sending phishing tests with attachments on them. (Recommended)
    5. \n
  5. Click Add.
    6. \n
  6. Click Save.\u00a0There may be a 20-minute delay for the changes to take place.
    7. \n
\n
\nNote: After following this article, we recommend setting up a test phishing campaign to 1-2 users to ensure your whitelisting was successful. As a last resource, we suggest reaching out to your service provider for assistance. Visit the Third-Party Whitelisting Assistance section of our Whitelisting Data and Anti-Spam Information article for an email template you can send to your service provider.
\n
\nTip: To ensure that you have whitelisted correctly, see our How to Verify You Have Whitelisted KnowBe4 Correctly article.

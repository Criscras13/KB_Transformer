In your Account Management console, you can find the Phishing Templates subtab under the Phishing tab. This subtab allows you to search through our System Templates selection to meet your managed accounts' needs. To learn more about this subtab, see the sections below. You can also exclude system templates from your managed phishing campaigns, manage templates, create new templates, and manage template topics.
\n
Managed Templates
\n
In the Managed Templates section of the Phishing Templates subtab, you can search through your templates and manage your template topics.
\n
Searching for Your Templates
\n
In the Managed Templates section, you can use filters to search for your templates. For more information about these filters, see the screenshot and list below.
\n
    \n
  1. \nTopics: Select the phishing template topics you want to use. You can select one or more phishing template topics. By default, all template topics are selected, and your templates will be random.
    2. \n
  2. \nLanguages: You can use this filter to find localized templates in the selected languages.
    3. \n
  3. \nDifficulty: You can use this filter to find templates with the selected difficulty ratings. For more information, see our Template Difficulty Ratings Overview article.
    4. \n
  4. \nSearch: You can use this filter to find templates with a specific keyword in the title.
    5. \n
\n
Viewing Your Search Results
\n
As you select filters in the Managed Templates section, the templates that meet your criteria will be displayed in the results table. This table shows the template's name, when it was last updated, language, difficulty rating, and topics. You can also preview templates and clear your filters. For more information, see the screenshot and list below: 
\n
    \n
  1. \nClear Filters: You can click this link to remove all of your selected filters and reset the template list.
    2. \n
  2. \nPreview: You can click the eye icon to preview the template.
    3. \n
\n
System Templates
\n
In the System Templates section of the Phishing Templates subtab, you can search through system templates by using filters and clone templates for your managed phishing campaigns.
\n
Searching for System Templates
\n
See the screenshot and list below to learn how you can search for templates in the System Templates section: 
\n
    \n
  1. \nTopics: Select the phishing template topics you want to use. You can select one or more phishing template topics.
    2. \n
  2. \nLanguages: You can use this filter to find localized templates in the selected languages.
    3. \n
  3. \nDifficulty: You can use this filter to find templates that have the selected difficulty ratings. For more information, see our Template Difficulty Ratings Overview article.
    4. \n
  4. \nSearch: You can use this filter to find templates with a specific keyword in the title.
    5. \n
  5. \nAttack Vectors: You can use this filter to find templates that include specific attack vectors, such as data entry templates. To view this filter, you'll need to click the + Additional Filters button.
    6. \n
  6. \nTemplate Features: You can use this filter to find templates with specific features, such as fake attachments. To view this filter, you'll need to click the + Additional Filters button.
    7. \n
  7. \nPlaceholders: You can use this filter to find templates that contain specific placeholders, such as the Company Name placeholder. To view this filter, you'll need to click the + Additional Filters button.
    8. \n
  8. \nRed Flags: You can use this filter to find templates that contain specific red flags, such as the Aggressive Content red flag. To view this filter, you'll need to click the + Additional Filters button.
    9. \n
\n
Viewing Your Search Results
\n
After selecting your filters, you\u2019ll see a list of templates that meet your criteria in the results table. Here, you can view information about the template, including the template\u2019s name, topics, language, difficulty rating, when it was last updated, and origin. You can also clone templates, preview templates, and clear your filters.
\n
For more information, see the screenshot and list below: 
\n
    \n
  1. \nClear Filters: You can click this link to remove all of your selected filters and reset the template list.
    2. \n
  2. \nPreview: You can click the eye icon to preview the template.
    3. \n
\n
Cloning Templates for Use
\n
On the Phishing Templates subtab, you can clone a template to use in your managed phishing campaigns. When you clone a template, it will automatically be saved to the Managed Templates section of the Phishing Templates subtab.
\n
To clone a template for use, follow the steps below:
\n
    \n
  1. Log in to your KSAT console.
    2. \n
  2. Navigate to Phishing > Phishing Templates.
    3. \n
  3. Select the check box next to the template or templates you want to clone. Then, click Clone Selected Templates.   \n
    4. \n
  4. The Clone Selected Templates pop-up will open. Select a language and topic for the templates.
    5. \n
  5. Once you've made your selections, click Clone at the bottom of the pop-up window.   \n
    6. \n
  6. These templates will now appear in the Managed Templates section.
    7. \n
\n
\n
\nNote:If you would like to copy a custom template, you'll need to copy the source code and paste it into a new template using the Source button.
\n
\n
Creating Managed Phishing Campaigns with Template Topics
\n
When you create phishing campaigns, you'll have access to an updated New Managed Phishing Campaign page, which includes a Template Topics drop-down menu. This drop-down menu allows you to select topics for your phishing templates instead of selecting Template Categories.
\n
\n
There is also a new Template Selection drop-down section where you can select how your users will receive their emails. Your choices are from Random, Full Random, and AIDA Selected. To learn more about Template Selection options, see our Automated Template Selection Overview article.
\n
This section also includes an Available Templates drop-down subsection, which you can expand to view the list of templates that will be sent to your users. From this list, you can view the languages that the templates are available in and preview them.
\n
You can also set your organization\u2019s template languages using the Set Template Language setting. Use this check box to select the languages you want to use in this phishing campaign. If multiple languages are selected, users will receive templates in any of the selected languages. This setting will override the account\u2019s default phishing language and the language set in the user\u2019s profile. When this check box is selected, the Language Override Selection drop-down menu will appear. You can select a maximum of five languages.
\n
\n
\n
\nImportant:For templates to populate in this section, you'll need to select all users or at least one group to enroll in the campaign.
\n
\n
For more details about how to customize phishing email templates, see our Create and Edit Email Templates and Landing Pages article or watch our Customization of Phishing Templates and Landing Pages video.
\n
\n
\nNote:You can also export email templates you've created and import them to specific managed accounts. You can use this feature if you want to add certain templates to only specific accounts or if one of your accounts makes a template you would like to use in other accounts. For more information, visit our Partner and Multi-Account: Use the Import and Export Feature article.
\n

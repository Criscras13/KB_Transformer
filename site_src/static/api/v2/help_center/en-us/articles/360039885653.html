If you're using Cisco Ironport spam filtering, you can whitelist KnowBe4 to allow our simulated phishing test emails and training notifications through to your end users.
\n
The instructions below include information from the\u00a0Cisco whitelisting article. If you run into issues whitelisting KnowBe4 in Cisco Ironport,\u00a0we recommend reaching out to Cisco for specific instructions. You can also\u00a0contact our support team whenever you need assistance.
\n\n
Whitelisting Cisco Ironport
\n
To whitelist KnowBe4 in Cisco Ironport, do the following:
\n
    \n
  1. From the Cisco Ironport admin console, navigate to the Mail Policies tab.
    2. \n
  2. Select HAT Overview. Please ensure that\u00a0InboundMail lister is selected.
    3. \n
  3. Click WHITELIST. If you do not see\u00a0WHITELIST, you can create your own group named \"WHITELIST\".
    4. \n
  4. Click Add Sender and add our IPs or hostnames. For the most up-to-date list of our IP addresses and hostnames, see our Whitelisting Data and Anti-Spam Information article.
    5. \n
  5. Click Submit and then Commit Changes.
    6. \n
\n
\nNote:After following this article, we recommend setting up a test phishing campaign to 1-2 users to ensure your whitelisting was successful. As a last resource, we suggest reaching out to your service \u00a0provider for assistance. Visit here for an email template you can send to your service provider.
\n\n\n
Skipping Outbreak Filter Scanning
\n
The instructions above for whitelisting Cisco Ironport do not prevent Ironport's Outbreak Filter from scanning emails from our IPs or hostnames. If you are experiencing issues with our emails being quarantined, you may also need to set our IPs or hostnames to bypass this filter.\u00a0
\n
To skip Outbreak Filter Scanning, do the following:
\n
    \n
  1. From your Cisco Ironport admin console, navigate to the Mail Policies tab.
    2. \n
  2. Under the Message Modification section, enter our IP addresses or hostnames in the Bypass Domain Scanning table. For the most up-to-date list of our IP addresses and hostnames, see our Whitelisting Data and Anti-Spam Filtering Information article.
    3. \n
  3. Click Submit and then Commit Changes.
    4. \n
\n\n\n
Troubleshooting
\n
If Cisco Ironport is flagging KnowBe4's simulating phishing emails as spam or removing attachments from these emails, you may need to troubleshoot further in Cisco Ironport.
\n
For a potential troubleshooting method, see the steps below. If you don't see the solution you're looking for, we recommend reaching out to Cisco Ironport for assistance.
\n
    \n
  1. Create an individual HAT Mail Flow Policy specifically for KnowBe4.
    2. \n
  2. In this policy, disable Spam Detection and\u00a0Virus Protection. For more information, see Cisco Ironport's Mail Flow Policy documentation.\n
    3. \n
  3. Add a sender group for our IP addresses or hostnames. For the most up-to-date list of our IP addresses and hostnames, see our Whitelisting Data and Anti-Spam Filtering Information article.
    4. \n
  4. Apply the policy to the sender group. For more information, see Cisco Ironport's Message Handing documentation.
    5. \n
\n
\nTip: To ensure that you have whitelisted correctly, see our How to Verify You Have Whitelisted KnowBe4 Correctly article.

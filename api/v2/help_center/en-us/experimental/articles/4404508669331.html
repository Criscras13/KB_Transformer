In your KCM GRC Risk Management module, you can assess, manage, and monitor risks by adding them to your Risk Register. You can add risks individually, or if your organization has already identified a list of risks, you can use a CSV file to import multiple risks at once. For general information about using the Risk Register, see our How to Use Your Risk Register article.
\n
See the sections below to learn how to format your CSV file and import risks to your Risk Register.\u00a0
\n
\n
\nTip:If you're just getting started with your risk management program, we recommend using the Risk Wizard instead of manually adding risks. For more information, see our How to Use the Risk Wizard article.
\n
\n
Formatting the CSV File
\n
Before you import risks into your Risk Register, you'll need to create a CSV file that contains information about each risk. The CSV file can contain up to 500 rows of data.\u00a0
\n
\n
\nImportant:If you're using Microsoft Excel, you must save the file in the \"CSV UTF-8 (Comma delimited)\" format.
\n
\n

Importing the CSV File
\n
After you save your CSV file, you can import it into your platform. To import your CSV file, follow the steps below:\u00a0
\n
    \n
  1. Log in to your KCM GRC platform.
    2. \n
  2. Navigate to Risk Management > Risk Register\u00a0from the navigation panel.\u00a0
    3. \n
  3. At the top-right corner of the page, click the Import Risks\u00a0button. When you click this button, an\u00a0Import CSV Risks pop-up window will open.
    4. \n
  4. Click the Click to Upload\u00a0button.\u00a0
    5. \n
  5. Select the CSV file that contains your risk information. Once you select this file, the risks included in the import will display.\n
    \n
    \nTip:To remove risks from the import, click the trash can icon.
    \n
    \n
    6. \n
  6. Click the\u00a0Save Imported Risks\u00a0button.

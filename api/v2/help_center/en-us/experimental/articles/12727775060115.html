In this article, you'll learn about the Account Information section of your KSAT account settings. In the Account Information section, you can review your subscription details, update your privacy settings, update your organization\u2019s information, and more.
\n
Purchased Products and Add-ons
\n
In the Purchased Products and Add-ons section, you can view information about the KnowBe4 products and add-ons you\u2019ve purchased. This information includes the following information about your KSAT account:
\n
    \n
  • Your subscription level.
    • \n
  • Your subscription end date.
    • \n
  • The number of seats available on your account.
    • \n
  • The number of active users on your account.
    • \n
\n
This section also includes your subscription end date for other products and add-ons you\u2019ve purchased such as PhishER, CompliancePlus, KnowBe4 Student Edition, PasswordIQ, and SecurityCoach.
\n
\n
\nTip: If you\u2019d like to change your subscription details, contact your Account Manager or Customer Success Manager.
\n
\n
Account Privacy
\n
In the Account Privacy section, you can check if KnowBe4 agents have access to your account and change your privacy settings to your liking.
\n
To change your account privacy settings, click the Allow KnowBe4 agents to access this account drop-down menu. To temporarily grant access to your account, select your preferred time period from the drop-down menu. We recommend that you select Allow continual access to allow KnowBe4 agents to access your account. Granting access to KnowBe4 agents allows us to access your account and assist you if you experience technical issues in your account.
\n
The blue banner at the top of the section displays the expiry date of the account access. Once the access expires, your setting will be automatically updated to Do not allow access.
\n
If you select Do not allow access, our support team will not be able to access your account. If you don\u2019t allow access, you may experience some delays if our support team needs to view your account to help solve a technical issue.
\n
For more information about account privacy settings, see our Grant Support Access to Your Account article.
\n
Organization Information
\n
In the Organization Information section, you can update your organization\u2019s name, business hours, time zone, and other information about your organization.
\n
KSAT uses placeholders to populate this information into features such as phishing templates, landing pages, and training notifications. For more information, see our Placeholders Guide.
\n
See below for information about specific settings in this section:
\n
\n
    \n
  1. \nAllowed Domains: If your email environment includes multiple email domains, you can click Allowed Domains to add more subdomains or root domains to your KnowBe4 account. For more information, see our Add and Verify Domains article.
    2. \n
  2. \nBusiness Hours: Click the clock icon to add your organization\u2019s default business hours. This setting determines the default time period that PSTs and time-sensitive training notifications will be sent. It also determines the default start time for training campaigns. You can set different business hours or a different start time when creating a campaign.
    3. \n
  3. \nBusiness Days: Click the corresponding button for each of your organization\u2019s default business days. This setting determines the default start date when creating a phishing campaign. You can select a different start time when creating phishing or training campaigns.
    4. \n
  4. \nTime Zone: Click the drop-down menu and select your organization\u2019s default time zone. For more information about managing time zones in your console, see our Manage Your Time Zones article.
    5. \n
  5. \nDefault Admin Console Language: Click the drop-down menu and select a default language for your admin console. The language you select will apply to the entire admin console unless an admin selects a different Admin Console Language. If a user\u2019s language is not available, the account\u2019s default language will be used. For more information, see our Localization Guide.
    6. \n
  6. \nDate and Time Format: Click the drop-down menu and select the default date and time format that you\u2019d like to use in the console. After you select a default date and time format, any new users that you import will see the default setting in their user profile. The date and time format options are listed below:\n
      \n
    • 12-hour
      MM/DD/YYYY hh:mm AM/PM
      Example: 01/31/2022 02:00 PM
      • \n
    • \n
      24-hour
      DD/MM/YYYY HH:mm
      Example: 31/01/2022 14:00
      \n
      • \n
    \n
    7. \n
\n
Environment Information
\n
In the Environment Information section, you can view and edit your responses to the Environment Survey available on your KnowBe4 console dashboard. This section will not be available in your account settings until you have submitted your responses to the survey. Additionally, you can return to the survey at any time by clicking Edit Account Information. You can also remove all responses that you have submitted by clicking Clear All Responses. For more information, see our Environment Survey Overview article.
\n
Branding
\n
In the Branding section, you can customize your organization\u2019s branded content. We recommend that you use branded content to provide a familiar learning environment for your users.
\n
See below for more information about the settings in this section:
\n
\n
    \n
  1. \nCompany Logo URL: Enter the URL for your company logo to use the logo in templates. If you add your logo URL to the console, you can use the logo in phishing templates, landing pages, and training notifications. For information about logo requirements, see our Add Your Company Logo, Logo URL, and Brand Color to Your Console article.
    2. \n
  2. \nCompany Logo: Upload an image of your company logo to personalize your users\u2019 Learner Experience. This logo will replace the KnowBe4 logo at the top-left of the page when users log in to the Learner Experience. For information about logo requirements, see our Add Your Company Logo, Logo URL, and Brand Color to Your Console\u00a0article.
    3. \n
  3. \nBrand Color: Select a theme color for your Learner Experience. We recommend that you match the color to your organization's brand or logo color.
    4. \n
  4. \nUpload Branded Certificate: This setting allows you to design and upload a custom branded template for your certificates. If you upload a branded certificate, you can click Preview Current Certificate to view the certificate. For more information, see our Create a Branded Training Certificate\u00a0article.
    5. \n
  5. \n+ Placeholders: Click this button to add a custom banner, prefix, or signature block to your phishing emails. You can use these elements to customize phishing emails to match your organization\u2019s brand. This can also help when your banners, prefixes, or signatures no longer appear on phishing emails due to whitelisting rules. For more information, see our DMI Configuration Guide.
    6. \n
\n
Placeholders
\n
In the Placeholders section, you can customize specific placeholders for your organization. For example, you can customize placeholders to add a custom banner, prefix, or signature block to your phishing emails. This customization can also help when your banners, prefixes, or signatures no longer appear on phishing emails due to whitelisting rules. For more information, see our DMI Configuration Guide.
\n
To learn more about customizing placeholders, see the table below:
\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n
  Placeholder Drop-Down Menu Option    Description  
 Email Banner [[email_banner]] \n
 Allows you to set a custom email banner for phishing emails. 
\n
 You can also set the banner to automatically appear on all phishing emails or phishing emails delivered with Direct Message Injection (DMI).  
\n\n
 Subject Prefix [[subject_prefix]] \n
 Allows you to set a custom subject prefix for phishing emails. 
\n
 You can also set the subject prefix to automatically appear on all phishing emails or phishing emails delivered with DMI. 
\n
 Display Name [[display_name]] \n
Allows you to set how the display name placeholder populates in the console. You can choose from the four options below:
\n
    \n
  • \n[First Name] [Last Name]: Displays the user\u2019s first name and then the user\u2019s last name. This option is selected by default.
    • \n
  • \n[First Name]: Displays only the user\u2019s first name.
    • \n
  • \n[Last Name]: Displays only the user\u2019s last name.
    • \n
  • \n[Last Name] [First Name]: Displays the user\u2019s last name and then the user\u2019s first name.
    • \n
\n
If all related fields in the user\u2019s profile are blank, the display name placeholder will populate the user's email address instead.
\n
Signature Block [[signature]] \n\n
Allows you to set how the signature block placeholder populates in the console.
\n
Sender Name [[prt_sender_name]]\n
Allows you to set how the sender name placeholder populates in the console.
\n
Sender Email [[prt_sender_email]]\n
Allows you to set how the sender email placeholder populates in the console.

In this guide, you\u2019ll learn how to integrate Microsoft Teams with SecurityCoach. You can use Microsoft Teams to send SecurityTips to your users when risky activity is detected on their devices.
\n
For general information about SecurityCoach, see our SecurityCoach Product Manual. If you're looking for information about setting up security vendor integrations that will detect risky activity on your users' devices, see our vendor integration guides.
\n
\n
\nNote:Microsoft Government Community Cloud (GCC) and Government Community Cloud High (GCC-High) integrations are not supported at this time.
\n
\n
Setting Up the Integration
\n
To set up this integration, you must upload a SecurityCoach bot to Microsoft Teams. Depending on your organization's settings, you may also need to edit permissions to allow the application in Microsoft Teams.
\n
For more information, read the subsections below.
\n
Uploading the SecurityCoach Bot
\n
To add the SecurityCoach application to Microsoft Teams, follow the steps below:
\n
    \n
  1. Log in to your KSAT console.
    2. \n
  2. Navigate to SecurityCoach > Setup.
    3. \n
  3. From the menu on the left side of the page, select Delivery Setup.
    4. \n
  4. Click Microsoft Teams.
    5. \n
  5. \n
    Enter a name for the bot in the Bot Name (required) field.
    \n
    \n
    \nNote:The default name is KB4 Security Awareness. You can enter a different name for your bot in the Bot Name field.
    \n
    \n
    6. \n
  6. Click Upload Bot. When you click this button, a Microsoft 365 login page will display.
    7. \n
  7. Log in to your Microsoft 365 admin account. A new Permissions requested page will load after successful login.\n
      
    \n
      \n
    1. \nSign you in and read your profile: This permission is used to verify you have the correct admin permissions to manage the bot.
      2. \n
    2. \nSubmit application packages to your organization\u2019s catalog and cancel pending submissions: This permission is used to send data from SecurityCoach to the app catalog so that the bot can be deployed.
      3. \n
    3. \nRead and write to all app catalogs: This permission allows you to update and delete the bot from your SecurityCoach console.
      4. \n
    4. \nMaintain access to data you have given it access to: This permission allows the bot to maintain the other permissions until you delete the integration.
      5. \n
    \n
    8. \n
  8. Select the Consent on behalf of your organization check box, then click Accept at the bottom of the page.
    9. \n
  9. Back on the Microsoft Teams Setup page in SecurityCoach, click Review Permission. When you click this button, a Microsoft 365 login page will display.
    10. \n
  10. Log in to your Microsoft 365 admin account. A second Permissions requested page will load after successful login.\n
     
    \n
      \n
    1. \nRead all app catalogs: This permission confirms that the bot was successfully uploaded.
      2. \n
    2. \nRead all users\u2019 full profiles: This permission allows the bot to synchronize your selected Microsoft Teams users with SecurityCoach.
      3. \n
    3. \nRead directory data: This permission locates your Microsoft Teams users and installs the bot for them.
      4. \n
    4. \nAllow the app to manage itself for all users: This permission allows the bot to install, update, and uninstall itself for your users.
      5. \n
    5. \nAllow the Teams app to manage itself for all teams: This permission allows the bot to install, update, and uninstall itself for your teams.
      6. \n
    \n
    11. \n
  11. Click Accept at the bottom of the page.
    12. \n
  12. Back on the Microsoft Teams Setup page in SecurityCoach, enable the SecurityCoach bot for Specific Teams or All Teams Users in Microsoft Teams. If you are adding specific teams, select Specific Teams, choose your teams from the drop-down menu, then click Add Teams to confirm. If you are adding all Teams users, click All Teams Users, then click Add All Users to confirm.\n
      
    \n
    13. \n
  13. If your organization allows only specific applications in Microsoft Teams or if you encounter a policy permissions issue, continue to the steps in the Managing Setup Policies in Microsoft Teams section below. If your organization allows any applications in Microsoft Teams, continue to the Using the Integration section below.
    14. \n
\n
The Bot Has Been Blocked or Removed
\n
  
\n
If you encounter the error The bot has been blocked or removed, follow the steps below:
\n
    \n
  1. Log in to your Microsoft Teams admin center.
    2. \n
  2. Use the sidebar to navigate to the Teams app tab > Manage apps subtab.
    3. \n
  3. Look for the KB4 Security Awareness bot in the Manage apps subtab.\n
    \n
    \nImportant:The default name for the bot is KB4 Security Awareness. If you did not use the default name while following the steps in the Uploading the SecurityCoach Bot section, search for your custom bot name instead.
    \n
    \n
      \n
    • If the bot is present, permissions are still loading. Wait one minute and then refresh the Microsoft Teams setup page in your SecurityCoach console. If this does not resolve the error, follow the steps in the Deleting the Integration section and then set up the integration again.
      • \n
    • If the bot is not present, the bot was removed in Microsoft Teams. Follow the steps in Deleting the Integration section, then set up the integration again.
      • \n
    \n
    4. \n
\n
The SecurityCoach Bot Could Not Be Installed
\n
  
\n
If any users experience an error during syncing, you can click Generate CSV to download a list of users that were not synced and the error message for each user.
\n
Managing Setup Policies in Microsoft Teams
\n
If your organization manages how apps are made available to users with the Teams app, you\u2019ll need to allow the SecurityCoach application.
\n
To allow the SecurityCoach application, follow the steps below:
\n
    \n
  1. Log in to your Microsoft Teams admin center.
    2. \n
  2. In the sidebar on the left side, click Teams apps to expand a drop-down menu.\n
      
    \n
    3. \n
  3. Click Setup policies from the drop-down menu.\n
      
    \n
    4. \n
  4. On the App setup policies page, you will need to click the name of the policy your team is under. If your team is under the default global policy, click Global (Org-wide default). If your team is under a custom policy, click that custom policy\u2019s name.\n
      
    \n
    5. \n
  5. In the following Global (Org-wide default) or custom policy page, click Add apps. A new sidebar will open on the right side of the screen.\n
      
    \n
    6. \n
  6. Use the search bar in the sidebar on the right side of the screen to search for the KB4 Security Awareness app.\n
    \n
    \nImportant:The default name for the app is KB4 Security Awareness. If you did not use the default name while Uploading the SecurityCoach Bot, search for your custom bot name instead.
    \n
    \n
      
    \n
    7. \n
  7. \nKB4 Security Awareness will appear in a drop-down menu underneath the search bar.\n
      
    \n
    8. \n
  8. Click Select to the right of KB4 Security Awareness.\n
      
    \n
    9. \n
  9. Once the app is selected, click Add below.\n
      
    \n
    10. \n
  10. The KB4 Security Awareness app will now show under Installed apps.\n
      
    \n
    11. \n
\n
Once the KB4 Security Awareness app is installed, allow up to 12 hours for changes to apply in the Microsoft admin center.
\n
\n
\nNote:If the KB4 Security Awareness app takes longer than 12 hours to deploy, a conflicting policy may be preventing deployment. For assistance with conflicting custom policies in the Microsoft admin center, please contact KnowBe4 support at support.knowbe4.com.
\n
\n
Once changes have been applied, SecurityCoach will automatically sync the Microsoft Teams integration every 12 hours. You can also manually resync your users by following the steps below:
\n
    \n
  1. Log in to your KnowBe4 console.
    2. \n
  2. Navigate to SecurityCoach > SetUp.
    3. \n
  3. Click the Microsoft Teams vendor tile.
    4. \n
  4. On the Microsoft Teams Setup page, click the Run User Sync button at the bottom-right side of the User Sync Details section.
    5. \n
\n
Once the new sync is complete, the results will display under the User Sync Details section. If there were any errors, you can click Generate CSV to view more details.
\n
  
\n
Using the Integration
\n
Once you've uploaded the bot and allowed the SecurityCoach application in Microsoft Teams, you can create real-time coaching campaigns to send SecurityTips to your users. For more information about creating real-time coaching campaigns, see our Real-Time Coaching Campaigns Guide.
\n
To view and manage the Microsoft Teams integration in your KSAT console, navigate to SecurityCoach > Setup > Delivery Setup > Microsoft Teams. From this page, you can view a list of your teams, verify how many users the bot has been enabled for, send yourself a test message, and more.
\n
\n
\nNote:The Send Me a Test button will use your set training language for the test message. For more information, see the Training Language section of our Localization Guide.
\n
\n
Deleting the Integration
\n
If needed, you can delete your Microsoft Teams integration from SecurityCoach. To delete the integration, follow the steps below:
\n
    \n
  1. Log in to your KSAT console.
    2. \n
  2. Navigate to SecurityCoach > Setup.
    3. \n
  3. From the menu on the left side of the page, select Delivery Setup.
    4. \n
  4. Click Microsoft Teams.\n
    \n
    5. \n
  5. On the right side of the page, click Delete Integration.\n
    \n
    \nNote:Deleting this integration in SecurityCoach will also delete it from your Microsoft Teams admin console.
    \n
    \n
      
    \n
    6. \n
  6. In the pop-up window that opens, click Confirm. When you click this button, a Microsoft 365 login page will open.\n
    \n
    7. \n
  7. Log in with your Microsoft admin credentials. Once you log in, the integration will be deleted.
    8. \n

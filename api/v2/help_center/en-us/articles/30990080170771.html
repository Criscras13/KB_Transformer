AIDA Template Generation allows you to leverage generative AI to create custom phishing and callback phishing templates on demand based on your organization\u2019s specifications. These AIDA agents allow you to better control the difficulty of your templates by simply selecting the difficulty level of the templates or, more specifically, choosing the individual Social Engineering Indicators (SEI) in the templates. For more information, view our AIDA Template Generation video.
\n
\n
\nImportant: Adding these agents will enable our new phishing and callback phishing templates UI. For more information, see our Phishing Templates Guide.
\n
\n
AIDA is available on the KnowBe4 console as an add-on to your Diamond-level subscription.
\n
Creating Your Phishing Template
\n
You can generate AI-based phishing templates. Use the following steps to access the AIDA Phishing Template Generator:
\n
Log in to your KnowBe4 console and navigate to Phishing > Phishing Templates.
\n
    \n
  1. Click the My Templates subtab.
    2. \n
  2. Click the Create AIDA Template button.
    3. \n
  3. Fill out the fields on the Phishing Template Generator page.
    4. \n
\n
For more information on how to create a phishing template using AIDA, see the screenshot and list below.
\n
  
\n
    \n
  1. \nLanguage: Use this drop-down menu to select the language for your phishing template.
    2. \n
  2. \nText locale: In this field, enter your users\u2019 region or country to tailor the template text with the expressions, nuances, and cultural styles of their locale. Entering this information helps the template feel more authentic and relevant to your users.
    3. \n
  3. \nTone: Use this drop-down menu to select a tone for your phishing template.
    4. \n
  4. \nDescribe the template you would like to generate: In this field, enter a brief description of what kind of template you want AIDA to create. For more examples, see the Prompt Examples section below.
    5. \n
  5. \nWhich attack vector should be used?: In this section, select the type of attack vectors that you would like to include in your phishing template.
    6. \n
  6. \nSender and Reply-to: In this section, enter the email address and name of the sender. Also, enter the email and name for the reply-to email.
    7. \n
  7. \nTreat as Spoof: Selecting this checkbox treats this email as a spoof and marks it as a red flag.
    8. \n
  8. \nDifficulty: In this section, select a difficulty rating for this phishing template. For more information, see our Template Difficulty Ratings Overview article.
    9. \n
  9. \nPersonalization and Placeholders: In this section, select the type of placeholders you would like to use in your AIDA phishing template. You can choose options that are more personal or general, depending on your organization\u2019s preferences. Your placeholders are set in your Account Settings. For more information, see our Account Information Placeholders Guide.
    10. \n
  10. \nSave Template: In this section, enter a name for your phishing template.
    11. \n
\n
When you\u2019re finished, click Generate Template. Your AIDA phishing template will appear on the right side of the screen.
\n
Viewing and Editing Your Phishing Template
\n
After you\u2019ve created your AIDA phishing template, you can view it and edit it as needed to fit your organization. For more information, see the screenshot and list below.
\n
  
\n
    \n
  1. \nRegenerate: Select this button to create a new phishing template using the same prompts in the Creating Your Phishing Template section.
    2. \n
  2. \nPreview: Select this filter to preview your phishing template.
    3. \n
  3. \nEdit: Select this filter to edit your phishing template using the What You See Is What You Get (WYSIWYG) editor.
    4. \n
  4. \nShow Red Flags: Select this button to reveal the red flags in this phishing template. For more information, see the Where Can I Get a List of Red Flags to Use? section of our Social Engineering Indicators (SEI) Guide.
    5. \n
  5. \nLanding Page: If you selected a Link or QR Code attack vector when creating your phishing template, you will see a Landing Page section. Click this button to view the landing page that will be used for the phishing template.\n
    \n
    \nNote: When editing the template, you can manually select the landing page you want to use.
    \n
    \n
    6. \n
  6. \nHow did AIDA do?: Use the reaction buttons to rate AIDA\u2019s performance in creating a phishing template. You can provide feedback on whether AIDA performed poorly, performed okay, or performed great.
    7. \n
\n
When you\u2019re satisfied with your phishing template, click Finish and Save Template. You can view your AIDA phishing templates by navigating to Phishing Templates > My Templates. Templates created with AIDA will feature an AIDA tag.
\n
Prompt Examples
\n
You can use the examples below to help you generate phishing templates using AIDA. Feel free to copy and paste these prompts in the Describe the template you would like to generate field or use them as inspiration for your own unique prompt.
\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n
Use CasePrompt Example
IT SpoofMake a template that looks like it is coming from our organization\u2019s IT team, which tells the user they need to update their password before the end of the day, or they will no longer be able to log in to their account. Include a link for them to click. Make it sound very urgent and serious, but apologetic for the short timeline.
Business Email Compromise/CEO FraudMake a friendly-sounding template that looks like it is coming from our CEO. Have the CEO ask the user to please review the attached document they\u2019ve created and provide feedback as soon as possible. The CEO should also say they understand it is an unusual request, but they were told the user is very good at this kind of thing.
A coworker sharing a funny videoCreate a template that looks like it is coming from a coworker who shares a fake video link to what the coworker says is a very funny video. The link should look like it is taking the user to YouTube. Encourage the user to watch the video. The tone should be casual and funny.
Tickets for an upcoming concertCreate a template that looks like it\u2019s coming from a local radio, saying that the user has won tickets for an upcoming concert. The link should look like it\u2019s taking the user to the radio\u2019s website. Make it sound very time-sensitive, and that they have to click soon or risk losing the tickets.
Calendar event from a coworkerMake a template that appears like a calendar invite. This email should look like it\u2019s an automated message from the user\u2019s calendar app with a link to the invite.

\n

\n
    \n
  1. Log in to your KnowBe4 console and navigate to Callback Phishing > Email Templates.
    2. \n
  2. Click the My Templates subtab.
    3. \n
  3. Click the Create AIDA Template button.
    4. \n
  4. Fill out the fields on the Callback Phishing Template Generator page.
    5. \n
\n
For more information on creating a callback phishing template using AIDA, see the screenshot and list below.
\n
  
\n
    \n
  1. \nLanguage: Use this drop-down menu to select the language for your phishing template.
    2. \n
  2. \nText locale: In this field, enter your users\u2019 region or country to tailor the template text with the expressions, nuances, and cultural styles of their locale. Entering this information helps the template feel more authentic and relevant to your users.
    3. \n
  3. \nTone: Use this drop-down menu to select a tone for your phishing template.
    4. \n
  4. \nDescribe the template you would like to generate: In this field, enter a brief description of what kind of template you want AIDA to create. For more examples, see the Prompt Examples section above.
    5. \n
  5. \nSender: In this section, enter the email address and name of the sender.
    6. \n
  6. \nTreat as Spoof: Selecting this checkbox treats this email as a spoof and marks it as a red flag.
    7. \n
  7. \nDifficulty: In this section, select a difficulty rating for this phishing template. For more information, see our Template Difficulty Ratings Overview article.
    8. \n
  8. \nPersonalization and Placeholders: In this section, select the type of placeholders you would like to use in your AIDA phishing template. You can choose options that are more personal or general, depending on your organization\u2019s preferences. Your placeholders are set in your Account Settings. For more information, see our Account Information Placeholders Guide.
    9. \n
  9. \nSave Template: In this section, enter a name for your phishing template.
    10. \n
\n
When you\u2019re finished, click Generate Template. Your AIDA phishing template will appear on the right side of the screen.

Viewing and Editing Your Callback Phishing Template
\n
After you\u2019ve created your AIDA callback phishing template, you can view it and edit it as needed to fit your organization. For more information, see the screenshot and list below.
\n
  
\n
    \n
  1. \nRegenerate: Select this button to create a new phishing template using the same prompts in the Creating Your Phishing Template section.
    2. \n
  2. \nPreview: Select this filter to preview your phishing template.
    3. \n
  3. \nEdit: Select this filter to edit your phishing template using the What You See Is What You Get (WYSIWYG) editor.
    4. \n
  4. \nShow Red Flags: Select this button to reveal the red flags in this phishing template. For more information, see the Where Can I Get a List of Red Flags to Use? section of our Social Engineering Indicators (SEI) Guide.
    5. \n
  5. \nGreeting: This section displays the greeting message and language translation for the greeting. Click the Edit button at the top of the template to change the greeting and translation.
    6. \n
  6. \nResponse: This section displays the response message and language translation for the response. Click the Edit button at the top of the template to change the response and translation.\n
    \n
    \nImportant: You must select a greeting and response to save the template.
    \n
    \n
    7. \n
  7. \nHow did AIDA do?: Use the reaction buttons to rate AIDA\u2019s performance in creating a phishing template. You can provide feedback on whether AIDA performed poorly, performed okay, or performed great.
    8. \n
\n
When satisfied with your callback phishing template, click Finish and Save Template. You can view your AIDA callback phishing templates by navigating to Callback Phishing > Email Templates > My Templates. Templates created with AIDA will feature an AIDA tag.

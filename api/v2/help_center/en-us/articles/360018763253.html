Described below are the tags you\u2019ll find next to the title of templates and landing pages within your console.
\n
Phishing Templates
\n
The tags within the title of a phishing template typically denote the type of attack vector(s) included within the template.
\n
\n
\nNote:If you don't want your users to be tested with our various attachment types or if you want to change the domain the [[domain]] placeholder uses to something other than your own domain, you can modify these settings from the Phishing section of the Account Settings page. See this article to learn more.\u00a0
\n
\n
These phishing template tags include:
\n
    \n
  • \nLink - This means there is a link (a hyperlink) on this phishing template that if the user clicks on it, they fail the phishing test.
    • \n
  • \nSpoofs Domain - This means this phishing template appears to come from the user\u2019s domain. KnowBe4 is able to \u201cspoof\u201d a user\u2019s domain by using a placeholder [[domain]]. If do not want the email to spoof your domain, you can use the Overwrite [[domain]] Placeholder option in your account settings to use a domain of your choice instead. For more information, see the Phishing section of our Account Settings article.\u00a0
    • \n
  • \nFake Attachment - This means the template uses an image that appears to be an attachment but is not. The fake attachment is also a link. If\u00a0the user clicks on it, they fail the phishing test.
    • \n
  • \nExcel Attachment - This means there is a Microsoft Excel attachment on the template.
    • \n
  • \nPowerPoint Attachment -\u00a0This means there is a Microsoft PowerPoint attachment on the template.

    • \n

Notification Templates
\n
The tags within the title of the notification templates denote what type of content to expect within the body of the notification template or the intended audience. These tags include:
\n
    \n
  • \nFrom KnowBe4\u00a0- These templates use the KnowBe4 logo and name and are sent from a KnowBe4 email address.
    • \n
  • \nLogo\u00a0- These templates use your organization's logo instead of the KnowBe4 logo. For information on adding your organization's logo, here is our article on how to add your logo.
    • \n
  • \nFrom Company Domain\u00a0- These templates use the placeholder [[domain]] so the email will appear to be coming from your organization's domain.
    • \n
  • \nManager\u00a0- These templates are intended for managers.
    • \n
  • \nAdmin\u00a0- These templates are intended for a KnowBe4 admin.
    • \n
  • \nRemedial Training\u00a0- These templates are for a user who requires remedial training.
    • \n
  • \nPassword-less\u00a0-These templates are for accounts that are using password-less logins. For more information on password-less logins, check out our How to Enable and Use Password-Less Logins article.
    • \n
  • \nPolicy\u00a0- These templates are specific to policy training.
    • \n
  • \nWith Instructional Video Link\u00a0- These templates include a link to a video from our Knowledge Base.
    • \n
  • \nCyber Hero\u00a0- These emails are specific for training if you've enabled gamification for your Learner Experience (LX). For more information on gamification, check out the Gamification section of our Learner Experience Guide.
    • \n
  • \nBanner - These templates include the KnowBe4 logo and a customizable banner of text across the top of the template.
    • \n
  • \nBorder - These templates include the KnowBe4 logo and a customizable, colored border.
    • \n
\n
Notification Template Categories Tags
\n
    \n
  • \nUser\u00a0- This category includes notifications intended for users.
    • \n
  • \nManager/Admins -\u00a0This category includes notifications intended for managers and admins.

Landing Pages
\n
The tags within the title of a landing page provide information about the landing page. These tags include:
\n
    \n
  • \nTranslatable\u00a0- This means that the landing page is localized and can be displayed in multiple languages. See this article for more information on localized landing pages.\u00a0
    • \n
  • \nDo Not Edit\u00a0- This means this template might not work as intended if the code is edited.
    • \n
  • \nUse With Caution - This means that the landing page may cause a user to panic and react before thinking. If you decide to use a landing page with this tag, we recommend informing your stakeholders and IT team first. This way, they can take any necessary precautions and prepare for user questions.
    • \n
  • \nEnglish\u00a0- This means this landing page is in English.
    • \n
  • \nSpanish\u00a0-\u00a0This means this landing page is in Spanish.
    • \n
  • \nPlatinum/Diamond Only\u00a0- This means the landing page is for Platinum and Diamond subscription levels only.
    • \n
  • \nBranded\u00a0- This means that these landing pages use your organization's logo and name.\u00a0For information on adding your organization's logo,\u00a0here\u00a0is our article on how to add your logo.
    • \n

Security Hints and Tips and Scam of the Week
\n
The tags within the title of these topics indicate that the Security Hints and Tips and Scam of the Week are \"Not PST\" and whether or not the templates within it are \"Branded\". Below are the definitions of what these tags mean:
\n
    \n
  • \nNot PST -\u00a0 This means that this topic is not meant to measure your users' phish-prone percentages and instead should be used for informational or educational purposes only. Topics with this tag do not contain templates with phishing links or any other attack methods on them.
    • \n
  • \nBranded - This means that the template is using your organization's logo and name.\u00a0For information on adding your organization's logo,\u00a0here\u00a0is our article on how to add your logo.
